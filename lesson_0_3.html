Pre-requisites: What You Need



Hardware

A popular misconception when it comes to programming is you need a super powerful, expensive computer to program, or need to have a specific operating system, or need to have a macbook covered in stickers from different startups. The truth is you can program, and do good programing, with some very simple hardware. Literally any laptop made in the past 5 or 10 years running windows, macOS, or Linux can be used to get started programming in python. Even the Raspberry Pi Zero, a linux based computer that costs literally $10 can run most of the python programs we’re gonna create in this course. And don’t worry you won't need a specific operating system there are versions of python for pretty much every platform although covering your laptop with stickers will greatly improve your ability to program.

Software

Again, as long as your running windows, macOS, or Linux, you’ll be fine. All the software dependencies you need will be explained in the course and will be available for free .

Motivation and Discipline

You know what else is free? Discipline. There’s really no secret to learning how to program other than discipline. Learning to program has been described as learning to rewire your brain by some people as it requires you to have a really solid grasp on logic. The only way to fully learn this is by practicing as often as possible. Going through tutorials, working on your own goals, and completing the code_otter exercises as regularly as possible is the best way to make sure you’re taking the full advantage of this course.
