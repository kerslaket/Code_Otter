What Are Datatypes and Variables



Pretty much every programming language, at least every Object-Oriented Programming language incorporates the use of both datatypes and variables. Datatypes are a way of describing a piece of data in programming, thus detailing ways you can manipulate it, store it, and what over ways you can interact with it. For example Strings are a datatype consisting of letters/characters, basically words and sentences that you can manipulate by combining different strings into longer sentences, and by stripping out individual letters. Integers are another common datatype that describes a whole number that you can then perform all kinds of arithmetic on, for example addition, subtraction,multiplication, modulus .etc.

Variables are a way of reserving a memory space to store a piece of data conforming to one of the above data types. You can then refer back to these variables for future use within your program.

In this section you will learn how to interact with and manipulate these datatypes and variables to your advantages.
